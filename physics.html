Kinematics

Distance: The total magnitude of displacement.

Speed: The rate of change in displacement over time.

Acceleration: The rate of change in disance over time.

Scalar Quantities Versus Vector Quantities

Scalar quantities only includes magnitude where as vector quantities include magnitude and direction.

Velocity: The rate of change in distance over time. The formula for average velocity is v = d√∑t. Since velocity is the rate of change in distance over time, to solve for average velocity, simple divide the distance by time, to get the average rate of change.

Displacement

Displacement: The amount of meters an object moved from its origin. The formula for displacement is d = vit + ½at2.

Acceleration

The acceleration of a free-falling object, remains constant.
